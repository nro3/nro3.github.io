New Daily COVID-19 Infection Trends



This chart shows the daily number of infections in Illinois as of July 30th. The large spike at the end of April and throughout May was when Illinois saw its highest rate of infections and deaths due to COVID-19. The government measures imposed in mid-March took a long time to take effect, but by the end of May the daily infections were decreased significantly. Unfortunately, Illinois is seeing a steady rise in daily infections again through July, most likely due to these stringent measures being relaxed. However, the rise is much slower than in May as shown by the difference in slope of the two blue trend lines, possibly due to the mandate requiring masks to be worn in public throughout Illinois.
